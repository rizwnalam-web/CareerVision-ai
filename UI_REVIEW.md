Predictive Roadmap
Current State: A timeline/GPS-style view titled "Predictive Roadmap — Visual GPS for AI & Machine Learning Engineer • Age 16," showing age-gated levels (Age 10: Level 1 "Scratch & Math" with tags Scratch / Basic Math, marked "Optimized"; Age 18: Level 2 "Bachelor's Level" with tags Python / Neural Networks). A right-rail "Intelligence Feed" shows an embedded video ("What is Artificial Intelligence?", 3M+ views) and a pull-quote stat about AI engineer demand growth. A "Sync Institutions" link appears under Level 1.
3.1 Observations
•	Page subtitle says "Age 16" but the first visible milestone is anchored at "AGE 10" — unclear whether age 16 is the user's current age, the start of the displayed range, or a typo/placeholder.
•	Level 1 ("Scratch & Math," age 10) is marked "OPTIMIZED" with a green checkmark, but for a 16-year-old user this milestone would already be in the past — it's unclear whether "Optimized" means "completed," "recommended," or "AI-tuned recommendation," and whether past milestones should even be shown as actionable.
•	The jump from Level 1 (Age 10, "Scratch & Math") to Level 2 (Age 18, "Bachelor's Level" / "CS Degree") skips ages 11–17 entirely — an 8-year gap with no intermediate milestones (e.g., high school coursework, portfolio projects, internships, standardized tests) feels sparse for a "predictive roadmap."
•	"SYNC INSTITUTIONS" appears only under Level 1 (age 10), where it's least relevant — institution-syncing is far more useful at the Bachelor's level (age 18) where it currently doesn't appear.
•	The Intelligence Feed video and quote ("AI Engineers are the fastest-growing tech role — 40% YoY demand surge in 2026") are identical across every module screenshot (Control, Roadmap, Global, Academy, Job Board) — it appears to be a static/hardcoded widget rather than contextual content.
•	A second right-rail card below the Intelligence Feed is empty in this view, later shown elsewhere as "Add monthly expenses to your financial profile to see breakdown" — financial nudges appear on the Roadmap page, which is a context mismatch.
•	Tags like "Scratch," "Basic Math," "Python," "Neural Networks" are shown as static pills with no links to courses, resources, or progress tracking (no checkboxes, no "mark complete").
3.2 Recommendations
•	[CRITICAL]  Clarify and fix the age framing — display the user's current age prominently and visually distinguish completed/past milestones from upcoming ones (e.g., greyed out + checkmark for past, highlighted for "you are here," outlined for future).
•	[CRITICAL]  Fill the 8-year gap between ages 10 and 18 with intermediate milestones (middle school exploration, high school course selection, AP/IB subjects, coding bootcamps, summer programs, first portfolio project, SAT/ACT or local equivalent, internship search) — this is the core value proposition of a "predictive roadmap" and currently feels incomplete.
•	[HIGH]  Move "Sync Institutions" to the Bachelor's level milestone (and any other milestone involving formal enrollment), and consider renaming for clarity, e.g., "Find Matching Universities."
•	[HIGH]  Make skill/subject tags actionable: clicking a tag (e.g., "Neural Networks") should open relevant Academy content or mark the topic as in-progress/completed, feeding back into the Control dashboard's Skills % metric.
•	[HIGH]  Replace the generic, repeated "What is Artificial Intelligence?" video with content contextual to the specific milestone being viewed (e.g., "How to choose a CS degree" when viewing the Bachelor's level).
•	[ENHANCEMENT]  Add a compact mini-map / progress overview at the top of the page showing all levels at a glance (e.g., Age 10 → 14 → 18 → 22 → 26) with the ability to jump to any level, rather than relying purely on vertical scroll.
•	[ENHANCEMENT]  Allow users to toggle between "Standard pace" and "Accelerated/Delayed pace" roadmap variants, since users discovering the platform at different ages need different starting points.
•	[ENHANCEMENT]  Remove the financial-profile nudge from this page's right rail — keep right-rail content focused on educational/career intelligence relevant to the roadmap.
